Getting to the Wedding, and Around San Francisco

We'll be arranging transportation from Union Square to the wedding venue.

To get around the city, we recommend Uber or Lyft. These apps allow you to quickly call a car to your location. Taxis are easy to find in some parts of the city, but not near the Park Chalet.

Public transit is very reliable for getting around downtown, Union Square, Mission District, and other neighborhoods in San Francisco. Visit 511.org for specific route information.

Things to Do While You're Here

Hop-on-hop-off Bus Tours - A convenient way to see the main attractions without a car.
Blazing Saddles Bike Rental - Bike across the Golden Gate Bridge, or around the SF Marina.
Foodie Walking Tours - Sample the City's great chocolate or other local treats.
Alcatraz - Book ahead for tours, or just take a bay cruise for amazing views of the city.
Cable Car Museum - A free look behind the scenes at the country's only mobile historic landmark. Take a cable car to get there.
Exploratorium - Fun interactive science museum. Great for kids!
Aquarium of the Bay - Along with several other attractions at Pier 39, a great stop for families.
Secrets of San Francisco - Via Magazine highlights lesser-known desinations in the City.

Our favorite hiking and walking spots: Angel Island, Land's End Park, Muir Woods, city hilltop parks.

Golden Gate Park has lots of attractions, in addition to great walking trails (and wedding venues!) : Japanese Tea Garden, Conservatory of Flowers, DeYoung Museum, California Academy of Sciences.

Our Story

Meeting

Cristi and Dave met at the Presidential Scholars recognition week in Washington D.C., during the summer of 2003. They had just graduated from high school and been accepted to Brown University. They quickly became friends.


At Brown the following fall, Cristi and Dave lived in the same freshman dorm building, and played poker and Settlers of Catan together with their friends. Cristi especially knew very few people entering Brown, and was always happy to see Dave's familiar face!

Dave studied computer science, advancing in the department and making many life-long friends, including all three of his groomsmen. Cristi deepened her love of language, choosing linguistics as a major, and writing for the College Hill Independent. At the end of sophomore year, their disparate groups of friends brought them back together by inviting them to rush events at Alpha Delta Phi, a coed fraternity. When they saw each other at these events, they both thought the same thing: "If a reasonable person like Dave/Cristi is doing this, it must be OK!", and tricked themselves into pledging.

Alpha Delta Phi

At Alpha Delta Phi, they had the opportunity to work together in new ways as they chaired events and became officers. Cristi was especially impressed by Dave's leadership qualities when he was elected president. Late in their senior year, Groomsman Bill would declare Cristi 'first lady'.

This might have been 'happily ever after' except that Dave was ready to move to San Francisco to take a job at Sun Microsystems, while Cristi was returning home to Oklahoma City to take an internship at Oklahoma Today magazine.


After College

They decided to spend the first two weeks of summer 2007 driving across the country to situate Dave in San Francisco. If 3,900 miles together didn't drive them apart, nothing could. They pulled into San Francisco two weeks later, and parked on Fair Oaks street, a few blocks from the apartment Dave would share with Stephanie, Adam, and Bill. The success of that road trip further cemented Dave and Cristi's feelings that they could live together, work together, and do anything together.


About two years later, they moved in together on the same little street where they'd found that first parking spot.

Proposal

In the fall of 2013, Dave started shopping for rings, and Cristi set up a wedding Pinterest page. One weekend in December, Dave suggested they go out at sunset, as they had done many times before for Cristi to sketch and Dave to photograph the landscape. They agreed on Bernal Hill, but a holiday party prevented them from going in the evening. Cristi unwittingly suggested sunrise instead.

On the morning of Saturday, December 12, they got up extremely early and drove to Bernal Hill in San Francisco. Cristi considered that Dave might be up to something, but his characeristically calm demeanor belied his intentions. When they got up the hill, Dave got down on one knee and asked her to marry him. Cristi immediately started nodding and crying, and didn't manage to actually say 'yes' until Dave repeatedly asked for confirmation.

They spent the rest of the morning drinking the champagne Dave had hidden on the hill the night before, and calling their families, who mostly asked what had taken so long.
